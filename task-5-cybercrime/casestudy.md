

## 🕵️ Crime Type: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Target** | First-year college students in India |
| **Platform** | WhatsApp, Instagram DMs |
| **Average Loss** | Rs. 500 – Rs. 5,000 per incident |
| **Recovery** | Nearly impossible — UPI transfers are irreversible |
| **Report** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) · Helpline **1930** |

---

## 📖 Case Study Summary

A first-year student (Priya) received a WhatsApp message from an unknown number posing as a senior student selling discounted textbooks. The scammer built trust using real course codes and professor names, created urgency ("only 2 copies left"), and requested an advance UPI payment of Rs. 950. After the transfer, they immediately blocked Priya across all platforms.

**How it happened — step by step:**
1. 🎭 **Trust building** — used real course/faculty info to appear legitimate
2. ⏱️ **Artificial urgency** — "another buyer is interested"
3. 💸 **Advance payment** — small amount to avoid suspicion
4. 🚫 **Disappear** — blocked immediately after transfer
